The Evolution of the Bicycle

"Life is like riding a bicycle. To keep your balance you must keep moving." - Albert Einstein

In 1817 Baron von Drais invented a walking machine that would hep him get around the royal gardens faster. Two same-size in-line wheels, the front one steerable, mounted in a frame upon which you straddled. The device was propelled by pushing your feet against the ground, thus rolling yourself and the device forward in a sort of gliding walk.
